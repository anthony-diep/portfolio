FORMOSAby. China Airlines

Branding | Packaging | App UI

theBRAND

Formosa is a sub-brand of China Airlines offering comparable experiences of its parent company at value pricing.

theGOAL

I wanted to develop an identity system that can fluidly exists in the China Airlines’ family, but distinct enough to have its present as a sub-brand and is able to connect to its target market.

Medium: Illustrator | Photoshop | XD

- LOGO -

COLOR SCHEME

FONTS

Peignot

Yananeska

CONCEPT

Since this is a sub-brand of China Airlines, I wanted to ensure that there is a connection between the logos. Upon researching China Airlines I found the logo is the national flower of Taiwan. I proceeded to research for national symbols of the country, and settled on the butterfly for its connection with flower. To take it a step further, I also combined elements of China Airlines’ logo in Formosa’s logo. The butterfly logo is abstracted for a modernized look to identify with the new generation of travelers.

- ADDITIONAL ASSETS -

PATTERNS

Patterns designed utilizing the brand identity of the butterfly, the parent company’s flower, and Taiwan’s mountainous geography.


BOARDING PASS

Boarding passes are designed vertically for a unique and modern style matching the brand’s core values.

NOTEPAD

LUGGAGE TAGS

Formosa luggage tags designed using the brand’s signature gradient and patterns.

IN-FLIGHT MENU

Modern minimally designed with the brand’s colors that is both pleasing on the eyes and easy to read.
